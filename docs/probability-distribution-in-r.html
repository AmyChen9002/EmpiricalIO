Chapter 4 Probability Distribution in R

4.1 Continuous Uniform Distribution

  • The continuous uniform distribution is the probability distribution of random number selection from the continuous interval between a and b. Its density function is defined by the following. \[ f(x) = \begin{cases} \frac{1}{b - a} &\text{ if } a \le x \le b,\\ 0 &\text{ otherwise.} \end{cases} \]

  • Problem: Draw ten random numbers between one and three.

  • Solution: We apply the generation function runif of the uniform distribution to generate ten random numbers between one and three.

runif(10, min = 1, max = 3)
##  [1] 2.206418 2.503880 1.219035 2.096578 1.620831 1.262546 1.902545
##  [8] 1.029421 1.371902 1.360510

  • Problem: What is the probability density of \(U[1, 3]\) at \(x = 2\)?

  • Solution: We apply the function dunif to obtain the value of the probability density function of the uniform distribution at a particular point.

dunif(2, min = 1, max = 3)
## [1] 0.5

  • Problem: What is the 1st quantile of \(U[1, 3]\)?

  • Solution: We apply the function qunif to obtain the quartile of the uniform distribution.

qunif(0.25, min = 1, max = 3)
## [1] 1.5

  • Problem: What is the probability that the random numbders from \(U[1, 3]\) is below 2?

  • Solution: We apply the function punif to obtain the probability disrtibution of the uniform distribution.

punif(2, min = 1, max = 3)
## [1] 0.5

4.2 Normal Distribution

  • The normal distribution is defined by the following probability density function, where \(\mu\) is the population mean and \(\sigma^2\) is the variance. The probability density function is: \[ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \sigma^2}} \exp^{- \frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}}. \]

  • Problem: Draw 100 random numbers from a normal distribution with mean 5 and standard deviation 1.

  • Solution: We apply the generation function rnrom of the normal distribution to generate 100 random numbers.

numbers <- rnorm(100, mean = 5, sd = 1)
